# Executive Summary

**Author:** Anuj Saini | **Date:** July 7, 2026

---

## Three Takeaways

**1. The business is healthy — but churn is predictable, not random.**  
Current MRR sits at approximately **$1.82 M**, anchored by the Premium tier ($1.25 M). Monthly churn rate is in the 2–5% range. Retention is strong: paid subscribers rarely fall below 80% even at 12 months. This is not a business in freefall — it is a business with a specific, identifiable vulnerability.

**2. The clearest churn signal is support friction, not product dissatisfaction.**  
Churned customers filed **67% more urgent/critical support tickets** than retained customers. Retained customers, conversely, created **62% more collaboration events** and **58% more data-export events** — clear signals of embedded product value. Churn is not happening because customers don't like the product; it is happening because a subset get stuck, escalate, and give up before the support team can rescue them.

**3. Act on behavioral signals first; use cohort data to design the follow-on.**  
The behavioral predictor findings (M4) are the higher-priority escalation: they are large in effect, low in implementation friction, and owned entirely by the customer success team. After those interventions are live, the cohort analysis (M3) provides the roadmap for a product-led Month-3 engagement program to pre-empt the late-stage churn cliff that every cohort hits at days 60–90.
